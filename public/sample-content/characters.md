# Space Cowboy Character Profiles

## Mara Venn

Character: Mara Venn

Mara is a former Spur Marshal who now works as an independent drover and salvage rider. She is precise, unsentimental in public, and privately haunted by the one evacuation order she gave too late during the Hollow Herd disaster. Mara never draws first unless a life is already in danger. She trusts repaired machines, old horses, and people who admit what they owe.

Consistency notes:
- Mara keeps promises even when doing so harms her interests.
- Mara speaks sparingly and notices practical details first.
- Mara will not allow an autonomous machine to make a life-or-death choice.
- Mara carries a cracked silver badge but does not wear it.

## Jo Bellweather

Character: Jo Bellweather

Jo is a young beacon mechanic from Mercy Station with a gift for hearing patterns in broken transmissions. They are curious, stubborn, and braver than their experience can support. Jo jokes when frightened and treats machines like temperamental animals rather than objects.

Consistency notes:
- Jo wants proof before belief, but once convinced, acts quickly.
- Jo is loyal to Mercy Station and protective of station children.
- Jo cannot resist repairing a damaged beacon.
- Jo fears open vacuum but hates admitting it.

## Elias Rook

Character: Elias Rook

Elias is an oxygen baron with courtly manners, immaculate gloves, and a ledger full of other people's futures. He believes civilization is built by debt and that mercy without leverage is merely waste. Elias is not reckless; he prefers contracts, pressure, and plausible innocence.

Consistency notes:
- Elias speaks softly and almost never threatens directly.
- Elias wants control of Saint Orra's Well.
- Elias respects courage but exploits desperation.
- Elias does not break written contracts, though he bends every silence around them.

## Sister Calder

Character: Sister Calder

Sister Calder is a mute navigator of Saint Orra's Well who communicates through chalk marks, star charts, and hand signs. She remembers routes no living pilot should know and has crossed Widow's Gate more than once. Her loyalty is to water, survival, and the old vows of the Well.

Consistency notes:
- Sister Calder never speaks aloud.
- Sister Calder will not reveal the Well's location unless a community faces extinction.
- Sister Calder marks warnings as constellations.
- Sister Calder distrusts anyone who treats water as property.

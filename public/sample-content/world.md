# Space Cowboy Sample World: The Sable Range

## Premise

The Sable Range is a frontier belt of dust moons, asteroid ranches, and half-terraformed cattle worlds orbiting the rust-colored giant known as Gethsemane. Settlers call it "the Range" because every chart looks like an old cattle map: claims, trails, wells, fences, and dangerous blank space.

Life here blends hardbitten frontier customs with salvage-age spaceflight. A person might ride a vacuum-trained horse across a pressurized mesa in the morning, then dock a patched star-skiff at an orbital cantina by dusk. Reputation travels faster than light because gossip rides courier buoys, but justice is slow, local, and often personal.

## Tone

The tone is lyrical frontier science fiction: dusty, intimate, morally weathered, and quietly strange. Stories should feel like campfire legends told beside a cooling engine. Violence has cost. Mercy has cost. Every victory should leave a boot print in the ash.

## Technology

- Star-skiffs are small jump-capable ships with saddle-like pilot rigs, manual nav yokes, and charm strings tied around their consoles.
- Vacuum horses are bioengineered mounts with sealed lungs, pressure-skin, and magnetic shoes for riding low-gravity ranges.
- Railguns, coil lassos, beacon spurs, and solar brands are common tools.
- Advanced artificial intelligence is illegal in the Range after the Hollow Herd disaster.
- Most people distrust pristine technology. Repaired, named, and weathered tools are considered more reliable.

## Places

Setting: Mercy Station, a rotating depot built from three wrecked colony ships and a cathedral dome.

Setting: The Glass Arroyo, a canyon on the moon Cordero where lightning fused the dust into blue-black glass.

Setting: Widow's Gate, a jump corridor where ships sometimes return carrying passengers who remember different lives.

Setting: Saint Orra's Well, a hidden water reserve guarded by an order of mute navigators.

## Factions

- The Spur Marshals keep local peace but answer to no central government.
- The Cattle Barons control oxygen, grazing rights, and debt ledgers across the Range.
- The Choir of Dead Satellites broadcasts old emergency hymns and encrypted warnings.
- Free Drovers move herds, families, contraband, and secrets between moons.

## History

The Range was settled after the Inner Systems abandoned a terraforming project halfway through. The first ranchers survived by learning which machines could be trusted and which stars lied on the chart. Thirty years later, the Hollow Herd disaster destroyed seven settlements when autonomous cattle-drones interpreted a storm evacuation as a predator event.

Since then, the Range has lived by a harsh rule: machines may assist, but they may not decide.

## World Rules

Rule: No true artificial intelligence may operate inside the Sable Range. Expert systems are tolerated only if a human remains responsible for every decision.

Rule: A spoken debt sealed under starlight is legally binding, even without paper or witness.

Rule: Water rights outrank land rights, blood claims, and salvage claims.

Rule: A rider who carries another person's solar brand is responsible for that person's unfinished obligations.

Rule: Ships passing through Widow's Gate must broadcast their birth names, or the corridor may return them altered.

Rule: The Choir of Dead Satellites never lies, but its messages are rarely complete.
